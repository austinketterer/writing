Suspiria was certainly a vibe, but that's about it. I found everything else about the movie to be quite lacking. 

To start with the good. The visuals were very cool, with the colors especially popping in almost every scene. The architecture was interesting and made almost every scene captivating to look at on its own. The music and visuals together capture the tone the movie tries to set, but that tone is quite monotonous. When our host mentioned there was an entire album composed of the soundtrack, the instant unanimous response was asking, "There's more than one song in this movie?" The main theme seems to repeat on loop in every single scene, pausing in a seemingly comedic fashion occasionally when a door is closed, only to start back up about five seconds later. 

Suzy, the main character, barely even deserves a name. The same thing could be said about most of the characters here. Calling them "girl 1," "girl 2," and "witch" doesn't lose much. Suzy is a ballerina, she goes to this dance academy in Germany, and she has no survival instincts at all. That's about as far as her character description goes. Her friend is a bit curious about the academy, and the witch is evil and likes to kill people or something. 

Immediately upon her arrival at the academy, she witnesses a girl shouting at the door and running through the forest in the pouring rain. She hears about the girl's murder the next day, but seemingly none of this fazes her. Even stranger events ensue, and at one point she realizes they are drugging her food, but she just throws it away and continues her stay at the academy. This leads to her investigating and eventually killing the witch. The end. Yep. That's about it. 

What do I think about the characters? Nothing really. Did I learn anything? No. Was there anything to think about? Not really. 

Was it a fun watch though? Kinda.
