# Movie Review Template Instructions

When provided with a **Movie Title**, generate a new markdown file named `[movie_title_in_snake_case].md` with the following structure.

All guidelines and reference materials should be formatted as **Markdown Blockquotes (`> `)**. This makes them visually distinct in the editor and easy to delete or type around.

---

## File Structure

### 1. Detailed Plot Summary (Spoilers Included)
* **Format:** Wrap this section in a blockquote block at the top of the file.
  * *Example:*
    ```markdown
    > **Plot Reference:**
    > American ballet dancer Suzy Bannion arrives at a prestigious dance academy...
    ```
* **Content:** A detailed, multi-paragraph breakdown of the entire plot from start to finish (including twists and the ending) to help the writer recall the whole movie.

### 2. Chronological Scene Breakdown
* **Format:** Each scene bullet point must be generated as its own **individual blockquote line**, with a blank line in between.
  * *Example:*
    ```markdown
    > **Timeline:** 1. Suzy arrives at the dance academy in Freiburg on a stormy night.

    [Write your thoughts here...]

    > **Timeline:** 2. Suzy witnesses Pat Hingle fleeing the academy in terror.
    ```
* **Content:** A chronological list of the sequence of events, providing roughly **5–10 bullets per hour** of the movie's runtime.

### 3. Themes & Analysis Topics
* **Format:** Wrap this section in a blockquote block at the bottom of the file.
  * *Example:*
    ```markdown
    > **Themes & Analysis Topics:**
    > - Visuals and Color Palette (hyper-saturated primary colors, Technicolor process)
    > - Soundtrack by Goblin (heavy rock score, whispering chants)
    ```
* **Content:** A list of major themes, cinematic techniques, or artistic aspects commonly discussed in relation to this movie. Do not include specific critics' opinions or reviews to avoid bias.
